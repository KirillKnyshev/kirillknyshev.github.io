Case Study

Cyclistic User Analysis & Strategy

Project Overview

As a junior data analyst at Cyclistic, a fictional bike-share company in Chicago, I was tasked with answering a key business question: How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently? The goal is to provide data-backed insights to help the Director of Marketing, Lily Moreno, design strategies to convert casual riders into profitable annual members.

Tools & Technology

SQL (BigQuery): Processed and cleaned over 5.7 million rows of historical trip data, including data merging (UNION) and handling missing April datasets.
Tableau: Designed an interactive executive dashboard featuring ride heatmaps, hourly usage trends, and seasonal analysis.
Spreadsheets: Performed initial data validation and documented cleaning steps for stakeholder transparency.

The Process

To ensure the data was clean, consistent, and ready for analysis, I followed these steps:

  • Data Consolidation: Merged 12 months of historical trip data (spanning over 5.7 million rows) into a single master table using SQL UNION ALL in Google BigQuery[cite: 1, 2].
  • Integrity Check: Identified a data gap for April 2023 where rows were missing; resolved this by performing a targeted INSERT from the original source CSV to restore seasonal accuracy.
  • Data Transformation: Created new columns for ride_length (calculated as the difference between start and end times) and day_of_week to enable time-based behavioral analysis.
  • Data Cleaning: Filtered out "False Starts" (rides under 60 seconds) and maintenance entries where start_station_name was missing or listed as a test station to prevent skewing the results.

Key Insights & Results

  • User Behavior: Identified that casual riders take significantly longer trips (leisure-focused) while members show consistent, shorter peaks during weekday commute hours.
  • Geographic Hotspots: Visualized high-density start stations for casual riders near the Chicago lakefront, providing prime locations for targeted marketing.
  • Seasonal Impact: Discovered a sharp decline in casual ridership during winter months, leading to a recommendation for spring-focused membership conversion campaigns.

Final Recommendations

Based on the analysis, here are the top three strategic recommendations to convert casual riders into annual members:

  • Digital Marketing at "Leisure Hotspots": Focus social media and email campaigns on the top-performing stations identified in the Ride Heatmap, specifically near the lakefront and tourist attractions where casual usage is highest.
  • Weekend-to-Weekday Membership Trials: Since casual riders peak on weekends, offer a "Weekend Member" trial that provides discounted weekday rates, encouraging them to test the bikes for daily commuting—the primary behavior of annual members[cite: 2].
  • Seasonal "Spring-Start" Promotions: Launch targeted conversion campaigns in late March and April to coincide with the identified seasonal surge in ridership, offering a "First Year Member" discount for new sign-ups during the spring transition[cite: 2].
